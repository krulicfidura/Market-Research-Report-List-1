<p><p>The consumer audio codec market is divided into two main types: analog and digital. Analog consumer audio codecs process audio signals in their continuous form, typically involving components like amplifiers and filters to enhance sound quality. In contrast, digital consumer audio codecs convert audio signals into a digital format, enabling compatibility with various digital devices and platforms. Digital codecs often offer superior compression and enhanced audio fidelity, catering to the growing demand for high-quality sound in digital media consumption.</p></p>
