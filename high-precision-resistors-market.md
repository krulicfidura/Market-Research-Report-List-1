Chip resistors are compact, surface-mounted devices known for their stability, accuracy, and suitability in modern electronic applications. They are widely used in circuits requiring space efficiency and reliability. Wirewound resistors, on the other hand, are constructed by winding a wire around a substrate, offering superior precision and a high power rating. These resistors are favored in applications demanding high accuracy and thermal stability, such as industrial and aerospace systems.</p></p>
