<p><p>Hepatitis Delta Virus (HDV) infection primarily manifests in two forms: acute and chronic hepatitis D. Acute hepatitis D occurs following a recent infection, often in those already infected with Hepatitis B, leading to symptoms like jaundice and fatigue. In contrast, chronic hepatitis D develops when HDV persists beyond six months, potentially resulting in severe liver complications such as cirrhosis and liver cancer. The market for HDV treatments targets both these infection types, addressing the need for effective management and therapies.</p></p>
