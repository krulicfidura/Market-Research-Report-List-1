<p><p>The eyeglass lens material market comprises several types, each with unique properties. Silicone hydrogel offers oxygen permeability and comfort for prolonged wear, making it popular in contact lenses. Fluorosilicone acrylate combines silicone and fluorine for enhanced wettability and clarity. Hydrogel provides moisture retention and comfort, though less breathable than silicone variants. PMMA, or polymethyl methacrylate, is a durable, lightweight plastic often used in lenses but lacks flexibility. "Others" includes emerging materials and specialty lenses catering to specific needs and preferences.</p></p>
